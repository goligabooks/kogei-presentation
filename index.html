Designing a Living Structure

Vision & Values

We are building a self-sustaining cultural enterprise that communicates humanism and enriches the meaning of daily life.

This is not a personal project—it’s a structure designed to outlast us.
It will grow by design, not by hustle.
It will run on systems, not personalities.
It will exist to serve, not just to survive.

  • Humanistic: Grounded in care, meaning, and attention
  • Durable: Built to evolve and continue beyond the founders
  • Process-driven: Runs on systems, not individuals
  • Cultural-first: Revenue is necessary, but meaning is the point

Core Activities

  • Media: Magazine, books, podcast, newsletter, YouTube
  • Sell: Annual Kyoto Fair (2026), online shop
  • Education: Online learning, workshops, lectures
  • Artist Editions: Collaborations with contemporary artists

Our Role as Founders

We are not the labor—we are the architects.

  • Build the team, not just the product
  • Set the tone, then step back
  • Focus on systems, values, and culture

First Step — October 2026 Fair

  • Kyoto venue secured
  • First revenue opportunity + proof of concept
  • Launch online shop in parallel
  • Begin hiring: project manager, logistics, communications

Mid-Term Vision — Building the Organization

  • Form small teams around each activity
  • Create standard operating procedures
  • Delegate operations and transition to executive oversight
  • Prepare groundwork for future space

Long-Term Vision — The Building

  • Cultural HQ: restaurant, shop, coworking, guesthouse
  • Not just headquarters, but a cultural anchor
  • Funded by investors, sustained by the ecosystem
  • Physical expression of a living, evolving structure
